# MemoryBread logo concepts — round 2

All eight concepts were generated with the built-in `imagegen` tool. The previous icons were deliberately not passed as visual references so this round would not stay anchored to the toast-character style.

Shared requirements: square macOS/app icon, strong silhouette at 16px, warm cream/deep cocoa/baking-orange palette, vector-friendly geometry, no mockup, no watermark, no glossy 3D, and no replacement of existing application assets.

## E — Uppercase MB

```text
Fuse the uppercase letters “M” and “B” into one compact geometric monogram. Give the top contour a subtle soft loaf-like arch without drawing a literal bread slice. Use a deep cocoa rounded-square tile, a warm-cream symbol, and one small orange counter. Avoid toast characters, bookmarks, ribbons, stacked slices, brain/circuit imagery, and generic bank or crypto styling.
```

## F — Open book

```text
Form an open book from exactly two broad cream pages; make their shared outer crown subtly recall a freshly baked loaf. Turn the center gutter into one orange rising path, suggesting accumulated context returning when needed. Use flat editorial vector shapes on terracotta. Avoid literal toast, bookmarks, ribbons, speech bubbles, and library or publishing-house clichés.
```

## G — Context dialogue

```text
Build a compact assistant symbol from exactly two interlocking rounded speech shapes. Their combined silhouette should hint at a soft baked loaf crown, while the negative space between them forms a protected memory capsule. Use cocoa and orange on warm cream. Avoid generic quotation marks, chain links, infinity symbols, faces, literal bread, books, and brain imagery.
```

## H — Local vault

```text
Create one stout rounded local-memory cabinet with a gently arched top and exactly three horizontal inset slots. Make the top slot orange as the active memory and the other two cream. The result should feel private, local, dependable, and always available. Avoid literal bread, locks, clouds, database cylinders, filing-app and server-rack clichés.
```

## I — Memory pixels

```text
Arrange exactly seven chunky rounded blocks into a compact asymmetric digital-memory symbol. Use one orange block as the active remembered fragment, with the rest in cream and cocoa on deep brown. Keep it contemporary rather than retro game art. Avoid QR codes, Space Invaders, controllers, Microsoft/Slack/Dropbox resemblance, and tiny circuitry.
```

## J — Lowercase mb

```text
Draw “mb” as one broad continuous custom lowercase stroke. Make the first hump of the m soft and human, and place one small orange memory seed inside the bowl of the b. Aim for an editorial design-studio monogram rather than a technology-company logo. Avoid infinity, glasses, moustache, headphones, chain links, and generic SaaS marks.
```

## K — Memory imprint

```text
Build one compact symbol from exactly three thick nested arch bands, like an imprint pressed into warm dough. Break the outer band once at the lower right and place one small orange square fragment in the gap, suggesting retrieval. Avoid literal bread, fingerprints, Wi-Fi, rainbows, targets, brain imagery, and generic wellness or fintech branding.
```

## L — Chinese 记

```text
Redraw the single character “记” as a bold custom geometric mark with thick rounded strokes, simplified for 16px while remaining recognizable to Chinese users. Integrate a tiny loaf-crown curve into the top of the right component. Use cream with one orange accent on deep cocoa. Avoid brush texture, restaurant, food-delivery, and generic red chop-stamp styling.
```

These are raster exploration drafts. The selected direction should be redrawn deterministically as SVG before exporting production PNG, ICNS, and ICO files.
